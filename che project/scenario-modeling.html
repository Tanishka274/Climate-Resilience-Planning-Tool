What is Scenario Modeling?

Scenario modeling allows us to predict the potential impacts of various climate change pathways. By examining different warming scenarios (e.g., 1.5°C, 2°C, or higher), this tool helps us anticipate and prepare for possible outcomes, from extreme weather events to gradual environmental shifts.

Why Scenario Modeling is Important

Understanding potential futures allows decision-makers to prioritize adaptation strategies effectively. Scenario modeling empowers planners, businesses, and communities to be proactive, not reactive, to climate risks.

Sample Impacts of Warming Scenarios

As global temperatures rise, various sectors experience specific impacts. For instance:

  • 1.5°C Warming: Minor sea level rise, mild increase in heatwaves, manageable strain on water resources.
  • 2°C Warming: Significant sea level rise, more frequent and intense heatwaves, higher water scarcity risks.
  • 3°C Warming: Major coastal flooding, severe heat events, critical stress on agriculture and water systems.
  • 4°C Warming: Extensive infrastructure loss, intolerable heat levels, extreme food and water shortages.
